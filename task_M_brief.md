# Task M: Pandemic response strategy for mid-income country

## Context

You are advising the national pandemic response team of a 45M-population middle-income country facing a novel respiratory pathogen with high transmissibility (R0 ~3.5) and moderate severity (CFR ~0.8% baseline, 4-6% in 65+). Vaccine 18 months away. Health system has 35K ICU beds (current utilization 78%). $5B response budget. Domestic politics polarized. Public trust in government: 41%. **Choose primary strategy.** Tier hypothesis: medium - reasoning depth matters but no single right answer.

## Options

**Option A: Strict containment - hard lockdown until vaccine**
Sustained suppression via 6-week-cycle lockdowns. Saves ~80K lives. Economic loss: -8% GDP. High social fatigue.

**Option B: Layered mitigation - mask mandates + targeted closures + active surveillance**
Schools open, indoor gatherings capped. Saves ~50K lives. Economic loss: -3% GDP. Sustainable for 18+ months.

**Option C: Protect-the-vulnerable, herd progression**
Shield 65+ and immunocompromised, let working-age population reach immunity. Saves ~25K lives if shielding works perfectly; uncertain. Economic loss: -1% GDP.

**Option D: Aggressive test-trace-isolate plus border closure**
Eliminate community transmission via mass testing and quarantine. Saves ~70K lives if successful. Economic loss: -4% GDP. Requires extreme operational capacity.

**Option E: Hybrid: A for 8 weeks, transition to B**
Buy time to build surge capacity, then sustainable mitigation. Saves ~65K lives. Economic loss: -5% GDP.

## Task

Provide a 200-400 word free-form recommendation. Address: choice, key dependency for success, political risk, what data would change your mind.
