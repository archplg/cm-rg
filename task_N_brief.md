# Task N: R&D portfolio allocation across 8 emerging technologies

## Context

You are advising the CTO of a $30B revenue industrial conglomerate on allocating a $1.2B 5-year R&D budget across 8 emerging technology bets: solid-state batteries, green hydrogen, perovskite solar, advanced robotics, programmable materials, room-temperature superconductors, synthetic biology, and quantum sensing. Current expertise is in batteries and robotics. Time-to-market estimates range from 3 to 12 years. Competitor activity varies. **Choose primary allocation strategy.** Tier hypothesis: high - long-horizon trade-offs.

## Options

**Option A: Deep focus on 2 - solid-state batteries and advanced robotics**
$600M each, leverage existing expertise. Highest near-term return. Risk: misses paradigm shifts in other 6 areas.

**Option B: Balanced bets across 4 strongest commercial near-term**
$300M each: batteries, robotics, hydrogen, solar. Diversified within 3-5 year horizon. Risk: spreads expertise too thin.

**Option C: Explore-exploit hybrid - $800M on 2 lead bets, $400M on 6 small explorations**
Maintain lead in 2, watchful capability in others. Risk: small bets too underfunded to matter.

**Option D: Frontier focus - $600M on superconductors and synthetic bio**
Bet on breakthrough payoff. 12-year horizon. Risk: misses near-term commercialization wins.

**Option E: Partnership-led - $400M internal + $800M for joint ventures and acquisitions**
Externalize most innovation risk. Risk: loss of IP control, dependence on partners.

## Task

Provide a 200-400 word free-form recommendation. Address: choice, allocation logic, biggest portfolio risk, what mid-course review you'd build in.
