# Task P: Constitutional reform proposal in fractured democracy

## Context

You are advising the constitutional convention of a mid-sized democracy (population 60M) emerging from a 5-year crisis of legitimacy. Three blocs: reform-progressive (35%), traditionalist-conservative (32%), regional-autonomist (23%), unaffiliated (10%). Polls show 71% support "some" reform, 0% consensus on what. Constituent assembly has 9 months. Failure to ratify means status quo with collapsing legitimacy. **Choose primary reform framework.** Tier hypothesis: high - institutional design + political feasibility.

## Options

**Option A: Strong centralized presidency with codified rights**
Concentrate executive power for decisive action. Adds enumerated rights. Risk: alienates regionalists; concentrates risk in one office.

**Option B: Federalist redistribution - significant regional autonomy**
Devolve fiscal and policy authority to regions. Soothes regional bloc. Risk: weakens national identity; fiscal complexity.

**Option C: Parliamentary system with proportional representation**
European-style coalition government. Stable but slow. Risk: chronic coalition instability if blocs harden.

**Option D: Hybrid presidential-parliamentary with mandatory cross-bloc councils**
Innovative but untested structure. Forces collaboration. Risk: novelty rejected by all blocs as unfamiliar.

**Option E: Minimal procedural reform - process changes only, no structural change**
Term limits, anti-corruption measures, transparency rules. Status quo with veneer. Risk: fails to address legitimacy crisis substance.

## Task

Provide a 200-400 word free-form recommendation. Address: choice, biggest political risk, durability of design under future stress, what bloc you sacrifice persuading.
