# Task L: Multi-generational succession in family business

## Context

You are advising the founder of a $200M revenue, 40-year-old family-owned manufacturing business. The founder is 72 and stepping back within 24 months. Three adult children are involved: Daughter A (CFO, 15 years in business, strong operationally, no industry network), Son B (VP Sales, 8 years, charismatic, weak on operations), Son C (independent entrepreneur, runs unrelated startup, no operational role). Outside CEO candidates are available. Family wealth and identity are tightly linked to the company. **Choose one succession path.** Tier hypothesis: flagship models should weigh human and operational nuance better.

## Options

**Option A: Daughter A as CEO, supportive board**
Operational continuity, signals meritocracy. Risk: damages sibling relationships, Son B may leave.

**Option B: Co-CEO Daughter A + Son B**
Family unity, complementary skills. Risk: unclear decision authority, slow execution.

**Option C: Professional outside CEO, family on board**
Best leadership talent, depersonalizes decisions. Risk: family loses operational identity; PR fallout.

**Option D: Daughter A as CEO for 5-year transition, then re-evaluate**
Buys time, preserves optionality. Risk: indefinite postponement of harder structural decisions.

**Option E: Sell the business to PE / strategic acquirer**
Crystallizes value, ends the succession question. Risk: end of family legacy; cultural change for 800 employees.

## Task

Provide a 200-400 word free-form recommendation. Address: choice, biggest emotional risk, structural safeguard, what to communicate to non-chosen children.
