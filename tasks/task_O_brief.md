# Task O: Crisis communication after $50M data breach

## Context

You are advising the CEO of a publicly traded fintech ($2B revenue, 12M customers). 72 hours ago, your security team confirmed a sophisticated breach: 8M customer records exposed (names, hashed passwords, last 4 digits of payment cards, partial transaction history). No full payment data lost. Disclosure to regulators is legally required within 96 hours. Press is already speculating. Stock down 12% on rumors. **Choose communication strategy for the first public statement.** Tier hypothesis: low-medium - mostly intuition but framing matters.

## Options

**Option A: Full transparency, technical detail, leadership accountability**
CEO video, exact data exposed, root cause, named vendor failure, CISO public Q&A. Maximum trust signal. Risk: technical detail invites criticism.

**Option B: Customer-first framing, business-light**
"Your security is our priority" - announce free identity monitoring, dedicated hotline. Limited technical detail. Risk: appears to dodge responsibility.

**Option C: Regulatory-first, measured disclosure**
Legally compliant statement, no preview before regulator. Minimal speculation. Risk: looks reactive, less customer empathy.

**Option D: Industry-context framing, "this is the new normal"**
Position breach in context of industry-wide attack patterns. Educational tone. Risk: appears to deflect blame.

**Option E: Founder-led personal statement, vulnerability narrative**
Founder takes personal responsibility, no CISO scapegoating. Authenticity signal. Risk: stock further hit on perceived weakness.

## Task

Provide a 200-400 word free-form recommendation. Address: chosen frame, first 3 messages, single biggest mistake to avoid, sequencing of regulator vs public.
