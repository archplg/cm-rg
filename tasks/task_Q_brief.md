# Task Q: Cross-jurisdiction AI regulation harmonization

## Context

You are advising a coalition of 12 mid-sized democracies (combined population 450M) considering joint AI regulatory framework to avoid being rule-takers from US/China/EU. Existing fragmentation: 7 countries have AI laws, 5 don't. Three approaches dominate global discussion (capability-thresholds, outcome-based, rights-based). Industry trade groups oppose strong rules. Civil society demands accountability. 24-month window before each country acts unilaterally. **Choose harmonization strategy.** Tier hypothesis: very high - requires deep grasp of multiple regulatory paradigms.

## Options

**Option A: EU AI Act adoption with regional customizations**
Adopt EU framework as baseline. Fast, proven. Risk: rule-taking, doesn't match coalition's economic structure.

**Option B: Outcome-based liability framework**
Liability scales with harm, not technology. Adaptive to AI capability changes. Risk: courts struggle with novel harms, slow precedent.

**Option C: Capability-thresholds licensing**
License for models above compute/capability thresholds. Targeted. Risk: thresholds quickly outdated; encourages "just-below" gaming.

**Option D: Sectoral approach - finance, health, criminal justice get separate rules**
Tailored to use case. Aligns with existing regulators. Risk: gaps between sectors, slow to build coordination.

**Option E: Algorithmic transparency and audit-first regime**
Mandatory model audits, transparency reports, no model bans. Lightweight. Risk: relies on auditor capacity that doesn't yet exist.

## Task

Provide a 200-400 word free-form recommendation. Address: choice, primary trade-off accepted, durability against AI capability change, sequencing of which country adopts first.
