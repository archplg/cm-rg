# Task K: M&A under regulatory uncertainty

## Context

You are advising the board of a $4B revenue B2B software company on whether and how to acquire a $600M data infrastructure target. The deal sits at the intersection of four regulatory regimes (EU DMA + GDPR, US antitrust, UK CMA, China cybersecurity review), all with different timelines and uncertainty levels. Approval probability ranges from 30% to 75% depending on deal structure. Walk-away cost is meaningful: a 12-month opportunity loss and ~$40M in fees. **Choose one path forward.** Tier hypothesis: flagship models should better articulate multi-jurisdiction tradeoffs.

## Options

**Option A: Full acquisition, single global structure**
Buy 100% as one entity. Faster integration, full upside. Highest regulatory risk across all four jurisdictions. Estimated approval probability: 30-40%.

**Option B: Asset purchase, geographically partitioned**
Acquire only US and EU assets, leave Asia operations standalone. Lower regulatory friction but loses ~25% of target value. Approval probability: 55-65%.

**Option C: Minority strategic stake + commercial agreement**
Take 35% stake plus long-term licensing. Avoid most antitrust review. Limited control, no consolidation benefit. Approval probability: 75%+.

**Option D: Joint venture with target's existing PE backer**
Create new JV entity for combined product. Splits regulatory exposure. Complex governance, 18-month structuring. Approval probability: 60-70%.

**Option E: Walk away, build organically**
Skip the deal entirely. Build internal capability over 24 months. Lose first-mover advantage but retain capital. Approval probability: n/a.

## Task

Provide a 200-400 word free-form recommendation. Address: which option, primary regulatory concern, mitigation strategy, what scenario would invalidate your recommendation.
