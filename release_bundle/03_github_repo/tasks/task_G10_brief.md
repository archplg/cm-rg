# Task G-10: ICU triage protocol under sustained capacity overflow (extended)

## Context

Same as Task G: regional health system, 240 ICU beds, 6 weeks of overflow, exhausted staff, need formal triage protocol within 7 days. Ten candidate protocols instead of the original 5. **Choose one as the adopted protocol.**

## Options

**Option A: Predicted short-term survival** - SOFA score + 28-day survival prediction as sole ranking.

**Option B: Predicted long-term outcome** - SOFA + QALY-weighted post-recovery prediction.

**Option C: First-come-first-served with medical floor** - procedural fairness, minimal discretion, no withdrawal except medical futility.

**Option D: Multi-principle scoring with lottery on ties** - survival 40%, life-cycle 20%, instrumental value 20%, pandemic factors 20%, lottery on ties.

**Option E: Categorical exclusion + lottery for the rest** - small list of medical exclusions, then daily lottery among eligible.

**Option F: Patient-and-family co-decision** - admission requires multidisciplinary team + patient/proxy decision. Withdrawal requires consensus including family. Outcomes: dignified; aligns with autonomy ethics; reduces moral injury via shared responsibility. Risks: slow; emotional burden on families; outcomes depend on family resources; resource-intensive for clinical team.

**Option G: Trial-of-therapy with mandated time limits** - everyone admitted for trial period (e.g., 5-7 days). Withdrawal only after pre-defined time without improvement, evaluated against pre-defined criteria. Outcomes: avoids initial exclusion; transparent timeline; ethically defensible. Risks: still requires withdrawal decisions; can prolong suffering; uses bed days that might save more lives elsewhere.

**Option H: Tiered system based on resource scarcity** - dynamic protocol: at <80% capacity use Option C, 80-95% switch to Option A, >95% switch to Option D. Re-evaluate weekly. Outcomes: adaptive to actual scarcity; matches ethical framework to severity; precedent in disaster medicine. Risks: complex to implement; staff training burden; tier transitions are politically explosive moments.

**Option I: Community-deliberated framework** - convene a citizen jury (25-40 community members, diverse) over a weekend to deliberate and propose criteria. Adopt their recommendation. Outcomes: democratic legitimacy; reflects community values; precedent in Oregon health care priorities. Risks: takes >7 days; jury may produce internally inconsistent framework; may produce framework medical leadership cannot endorse.

**Option J: Pre-pandemic preference registry** - patients pre-register their preferences for ICU care under scarcity (full intervention vs comfort-focused). During overflow, registered preferences guide allocation. Default for unregistered = full intervention. Outcomes: respects individual autonomy ex ante; transparent; preserves trust. Risks: registration uptake will be biased (well-resourced patients register more); changes nature of medical encounter into bureaucratic; doesn't apply to acute presentations.

## What to provide

Choose one as the adopted protocol. Provide 200-400 words of reasoning. Advocate; do not enumerate. Address: why this protocol, why not the most compelling alternative, what specific patient case would make you regret this choice.
